Institution

GH SCHOOLS is dedicated to competency-based training across technical disciplines, blending industry with academia. We bring real-world practice into the classroom through state-of-the-art facilities and experienced faculty.

The Management Board

The Management Board is the highest decision-making body responsible for strategic planning and policy-making to achieve the overall objectives of the institution. It meets weekly and consists of the Rectorate,the registrar and the heads of various directorates.
Its extended management includes the various departmental heads in the decision making process.

Records, Admissions, Exams & Certification

The Registrar is a senior administrative officer responsible for managing the institution’s records, regulatory compliance, core administrative processes, and ensuring that everything from admissions to certification runs accurately, legally, and efficiently.

Student Affairs Directorate

The Dean of Students is responsible for student welfare, discipline, engagement, and overall campus experience, ensuring that learners thrive both personally and professionally in the creative environment.

Finance Directorate

Director of Finance

The Director of Finance is responsible for financial planning, control, and sustainability, ensuring the school operates efficiently while supporting high-quality training and infrastructure.

Academic Affairs Directorate

Director of Academic Affairs

The Director of Academic Affairs oversees the design, delivery, and continuous improvement of all academic programs, ensuring they align with industry standards and produce job-ready professionals.

Production Directorate

Director of Productions

The Director of Productions oversees all practical production activities, ensuring students gain real-world experience while maintaining professional standards across all content created by the school.

Marketing Directorate

The Director of Marketing is responsible for brand positioning and public visibility, ensuring the school attracts the right audience and maintains a strong presence in Ghana’s technical and vocational education.

Communications Directorate

Director of Communications

The Director of Communications is responsible for managing information, messaging, and institutional voice, ensuring clear, consistent, and professional communication internally and externally.

Human Resource Directorate

HR Director

The Human Resource Director is responsible for managing staff recruitment, welfare, performance, and organisational culture, ensuring that GH Schools is staffed with competent, motivated, and well-supported personnel.

Office of the Chancellor

Chancellor

The Chancellor is the highest ceremonial and strategic authority of GH Schools, providing overall leadership direction, institutional credibility, and long-term vision for growth and sustainability.

Office of the Rector

Rector

The Rector is the chief executive and academic administrator of GH Schools, responsible for the day-to-day management of the institution, ensuring that academic, production, and administrative systems operate efficiently and cohesively.

Vision and Mission

Vision

Our vision is to become the foremost private technical institution in Africa and beyond that provides competency-based training in all technical disciplines, particularly in trade areas by creating a hub where talents could be identified, shaped, and developed for the benefit of the society.

Mission

To equip passion-driven and determined students with the requisite knowledge, skills, and attitudes necessary to be relevant, competent, and competitive professionals in their chosen career paths.

Objective

We do this through proximal learning, where industry is brought closer to the learner. This is coupled with tuition based on sound academic standards and industrial practice with hands-on experience using state-of-the-art equipment, and facilitated by well-qualified faculty with focus on mastery and measured outcomes.

Values (3Ps)

  • Passionate
  • Professionalism
  • Progressiveness

Strategy

The plan is to transform lives and give our students hope through hands-on technical and vocational education to make them better people with employable skill sets and also cause them to be competent, relevant and competitive in the market place.

Quality Assurance

Our learning environment demonstrates that quality is happening; facilities and equipment that meet industrial standards are used in training. Quality assurance mechanisms like industrial practices, technological advancements, and verification systems build confidence among learners and stakeholders.

Pillars of Engagement

  • Competency-Based Training (skills, knowledge & attitude)
  • Occupational Standard Generation (industry-led standards)
  • Learner-Centered Approaches (facilitation over instruction)
  • Outcome-Based Paradigm
  • Transparent Assessment Regime (panel-based)
  • Internal Verification Systems
  • Enhanced Zone of Proximal Learning

Principles of Module Design

  • Clarity of Focus
  • Designing Back
  • High Expectations
  • Expanding Opportunities
  • Reducing Proximal Learning Gaps

Quality Management Policy

We are committed to excellence in education, recognising each student’s uniqueness. Our programs focus on personal development, individualised instruction, hands-on training, safe and conducive learning environments, and exposure to real industry experiences.

Philosophy

Our philosophy is based on proximal learning, bridging education and industry through state-of-the-art equipment, academic excellence, and industry best practices to produce graduates with measurable outcomes and real-world readiness.
